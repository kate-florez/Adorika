Wouldn’t it be great if the image moved?
Get Ready to be Wooed by the Newest Ad Format:
Come in Cinemagraphs

What if there was an ad format that could hypnotize your audiences? There is such a format – Cinemagraphs. If haven’t yet heard about them, then it’s time to fill the gaps since the industry is marching into the future and, according to Adweek, Facebook is anticipated to woo its audiences with Cinemagraphs.

The secret behind Cinemagraphs is that they include both stillness and motion. Long story short, what you get in the end is a still picture with a small movement area inside. With the industry always moving forward, looking for new ways to reach audiences, there is no doubt that this new captivating GIF ad format will open up a lot of opportunities.

Harry Potter books ideas come true


We certainly very well remember the scene from the Harry Potter movie where he received the first ever photo of his parents. His emotions were overwhelming of course, and the fact his parents were moving in the photo made it seem reel. Apparently, some of the fans really wanted to bring the story into the real life. So, who came up with this gold idea? The credit goes to photographers Jamie Beck and Kevin Burg. They actually found that the audience response rate to these images is extremely high. Cinemagraphs are able to catch users’ eyes in seconds once they come to life. Some of the major brands are known already to be using Cinemagraphs as part of their marketing campaigns. Thanks to their creators, we can see the real revolution GIF formats went through. Regular GIF images have nothing to scream “WOW” about them. Their abilities are very narrow and their quality is also questionable.

Why the Cinemagraph will be popular

Cinemagraphs, on the other side, take a GIF image to a whole another level, increasing its popularity and making it possible to create a video autoplay, something that is expected to appear in social media newsfeeds more and more. They are anticipated to be used as a substitute to the videos we are so used to. In comparison to a usual video, it is easier to make users look at a Cinemagraph, rather than making them watch an entire video. How do you think Cinemagraphs will influence the advertising industry? Would you include them in your campaigns? For examples of Cinemagraphs please visit cinemagraphs.com or another trustworthy resource.
