Grabbing the Attention of Generation-Z: Key Tips


Generation Z includes those energetic and young minds born after 1995. They have their own behaviors and a belief system marketers need to understand if they plan to target Gen Z.

It turns out Gen Z hardly believes in the concept called “The American Dream.” What they want is for marketers to shape messages that portray reality rather than the perfect lifestyle. Thus, marketers are advised to drop the notion of the ideal – be it beauty or anything else – and concentrate on something unique, something that has an entrepreneurial spirit.

Generation-Z – what is it like?

The people of this generation are focused on real-time customer support, bringing solutions to any problem. Gen Z is an innovative and independent “technology friend” and is used to create its own ways and styles. The word “personalization” means a great deal to the Gen Z people. Marketers should keep this in mind when building their campaigns and design products and services that allow Gen Z to show its independence and strive for customization.

Influence of devices around us

One of the differences between Millennials and Gen Z is that the latter is surrounded with computers and technology from birth. The world without technology seems alien to the Gen Z people. They live and breathe high tech and online, sharing everything they can find on the road. This means that marketers should not only be able to grasp the full essence of technology, but swim in the technological world like fish, creating an online open dialogue with this new generation.

So to sum up the topic


Gen Z is quite flexible and receptive and includes a large portion of the population. Therefore, marketers who design their strategies with this generation in mind, improving their digital lifestyle, are bound to succeed amongst this dynamic, plus the innovative generation.
